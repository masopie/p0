What is Git anyway?!

Git, a local version control system, helps you track changes by encouraging the use of a "master" and branches. The master is the walking, talking, working, unblemished original that you integrate branches, or versions being worked on, into once they are themselves working and finished. Git lets you keep the master free from accidental bugs by keeping branches separate and using a "commit," or little summary/log of changes, each time you merge a branch with the master.
